An honest look at your alternatives

If you're an operator business shopping for help with your data, you have four realistic options — and we're only the right one for some of you. Here's the whole landscape, including when to pick someone else.

The landscape

Four ways to get your data handled

Real market pricing, based on our own competitive research across 20+ firms.

Enterprise BI consultancy
$15K–$40K per dashboard · $12K–$25K+/mo

Deep benches, big-name clients, polished delivery. Built for companies with data teams to hand off to — and budgets to match.

Pick this if: you're $100M+ with an IT department. For a 30-person operator, you're paying for overhead you'll never use.

Managed analytics / "fractional data team"
$3K–$12K/mo · often $50K–$150K/yr all-in

The closest thing to what we do — a growing field of firms offering an outsourced data team. Nearly all of them target funded tech, SaaS, and e-commerce companies, and most price per data source or per project, so costs creep as you grow.

Pick this if: you're a funded software company that speaks their language. If you run trucks, policies, patients, or pallets — they weren't built for you.

Freelancer / generalist consultant
$1K–$7K per dashboard · $75–$150/hr

Cheap to start and sometimes great. But you get a deliverable, not a system: no version control, no maintenance, no automation, and when they move on, nobody can support what they built.

Pick this if: you need one report, once. Not if the numbers have to be right every Monday.

Your software's built-in reporting
Included in what you already pay

Your POS, AMS, PMS, or ERP ships with dashboards. They're fine — for that one system. The operator problem is that your revenue lives in one tool, costs in another, and customers in a third, and built-in reporting can't join them.

Pick this if: one system truly runs your whole business. Most operators we meet have three or more that don't talk.

The specific firms

How we stack up against the names you'll find

If you search this space, these are the firms you'll run into. They're good at what they do — here's an honest read on when they're the better choice.

HelpWithMetrics

Flat published tiers ($3K/$5K/$7K per month) and a disciplined, governed approach to metrics — genuinely well done. Focused on tech and SaaS companies; lighter on operational automation.

Choose them if: you're a SaaS company that lives in MRR and churn. Choose us if: you run operations — and want automation, not just reporting, at roughly half the monthly price.

Datateer

A strong managed-analytics platform that has recently focused entirely on the construction industry. Pricing runs per data source ($10K setup + ~$1.5K/mo per source), which scales up fast as you add systems.

Choose them if: you're a construction firm wanting a construction-specific platform. Choose us if: you're in any other operator vertical, or want a flat fee that doesn't grow per source.

Mammoth Growth

A large, capable growth-analytics consultancy oriented around marketing and product analytics for funded companies. Project-scoped engagements at consultancy rates.

Choose them if: you need growth/marketing analytics at scale. Choose us if: your questions are about operations, cash, and labor — not funnels.

Full Send & other fractional data teams

A growing field of firms (Full Send, Adaptive, Blue Margin, LeapFrogBI, and more) renting you data-team hours. Most target mid-market and tech; most price custom; stack ownership varies by contract.

Choose them if: you want flexible senior data hours on tap. Choose us if: you want a fixed price, a system you own, and a firm that speaks operator — not startup.

What we won't claim

We use AI heavily to deliver enterprise-grade work at operator prices, and a human reviews everything it touches before you ever see it.
